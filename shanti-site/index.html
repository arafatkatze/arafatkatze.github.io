Symbols From India

The Land That Remembered Me

In India I gathered the symbols I now live by. The Om (ॐ) that hums beneath all sound. The lotus that blooms unstained from the mud. The Bodhi leaf of awakening. The river Ganga at dawn, carrying away what no longer served me. Each became a doorway home.

The Yoga Retreat

Breath as Medicine

For a season I rose before the sun to practice. Asana to open the body, pranayama to steady the storm, dhyana to meet the stillness underneath. Yoga did not ask me to become someone new — it returned me to who I was before the world taught me to be afraid.

Asana

Movement that frees the body's held grief.

Pranayama

Breath that regulates a frightened nervous system.

Dhyana

Meditation that meets the witness within.

Sacred Plant Pathways

Meeting the Shadow With Tenderness

Part of my transcendence came through intentional, ceremonial work with sacred plants — including cannabis and other entheogenic pathways held in prayer and reverence. In safe, supported settings, these medicines helped me loosen the grip of old trauma, forgive my younger self, and feel — at last — that I belonged to my own life.

I share this not as instruction but as testimony. Every soul's path is its own. Mine wound through these doorways, and they led me back to breath, to my son, and to peace.

🌱 A gentle note: I honor these as sacred, personal experiences pursued with care, intention, and support. This is reflection — not medical, legal, or how-to advice. Please walk your own path safely and lawfully.
