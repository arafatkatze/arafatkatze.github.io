Chapter I

Before the Shirt

I always admired art as a lurker, but the act of creation didn't seem like "my thing". I was horrible at painting growing up — it seemed like a chore, a waste of time. I begrudgingly did it a few times but it never appealed to me.

Somewhere in my early twenties, a small mix of sophistication, along with just a general sense of taste, got me into art. The first time I went to Europe, I remember walking for hours listening to music, looking around the city, and just being happy. Everything felt so much more colorful and precise and just how it should be.

For me, the first dimension of art is looking at something that inspires a visceral feeling inside of me. The second dimension is the story of what made it what it is.

Think of Van Gogh losing his mind in a mental asylum to make one of the greatest works of art.

Chapter II

A Gift That Became Something Else

I liked consuming but I wanted more. The only way to be a genuine admirer of beauty is to partake in the act of creation.

So I took out this white shirt and planned a special date night with M. I really liked her, so the long-term goal was to finish the shirt and gift it to her. Sadly that couldn't work out as we stopped dating eventually — our hearts were elsewhere.

So now I have this shirt that's a memory of someone that I really cared about at one point in time (and still do) and there's not much I can do with it.

"Hell is the suffering of being unable to love."

— Dostoevsky

It sounds like a twisty philosopher one-liner to make misery sound fancy, to craft a sad boy aesthetic. But I get it. I do get it. I was mopey but I wouldn't want to be too mopey. Maybe I could turn this around.

Chapter III

When Life Gives You a Half-Painted Shirt

What do you do? You either work on a relationship eulogy or you find other cool people and start painting on it with them.

So I kept painting this shirt and made it full of many easter eggs and people I know. I would just hang with people and ask them to paint with me.
